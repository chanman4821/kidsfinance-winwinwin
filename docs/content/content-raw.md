## LESSON L8: "Risk vs. Reward"
**ID:** l8-risk-vs-reward
**Age Band:** 10-14
**Duration:** 12 minutes
**Mentor:** 🦉 Owl Olivia
**Prerequisites:** l7-what-is-a-stock
**Chapter Tie-In:** Chapter 4, Beat 4-H
**Concept Tags:** risk, reward, diversification, risk-spectrum, volatility, tradeoffs
**Badge:** ⚖️ "Risk Thinker"

---

### OPENING DIALOGUE

🦉 Owl Olivia says:
> "Welcome back! 🦉 Here is the most important rule in all of investing — 
> and nobody taught it to me until I was very, very old.
> I'm going to teach it to you TODAY so you never make the mistake I once made.
> It's three words: RISK EQUALS REWARD.
> Every high return comes with high risk. Every promise of 'no risk, big gain' is a lie."

---

### LESSON CONTENT

**What is RISK?**

Risk is the chance that something doesn't go the way you hoped.

When you invest coins in a business:
- BEST CASE: the business thrives → your investment grows 📈
- WORST CASE: the business fails → you lose some or all your coins 📉

Risk is NOT knowing which will happen.

🦉 Owl Olivia says:
> "Risk isn't bad. Risk is REAL. Every investment has some.
> The question is never 'is there risk?' — it's always 'HOW MUCH risk?
> And is the potential REWARD worth it?'"

**What is REWARD?**

Reward is the gain you get IF the investment goes well.

High reward = lots of extra coins if it works out.
Low reward = a small, steady gain.

**The Risk/Reward Tradeoff — It's a SPECTRUM**

🟢 LOW RISK → LOW REWARD
- Example: Keeping coins in the Coinwood Bank Savings Jar
- Your coins are SAFE. You get a small, predictable interest payment.
- You will NOT get rich fast. But you will NOT lose your coins.

🟡 MEDIUM RISK → MEDIUM REWARD
- Example: Buying shares in Squirrel Sage's established Nut Trading Company
- She's been running it for years. It usually does well.
- Small chance of loss. Decent chance of steady growth.

🔴 HIGH RISK → HIGH REWARD (or HIGH LOSS)
- Example: Investing in a brand new stall that opened last week
- Might be the next big thing! Or might fail in a month.
- You could DOUBLE your coins. You could lose them all.

🦉 Owl Olivia says:
> "Neither end of the spectrum is 'good' or 'bad.' 
> The question is: can you AFFORD to lose this money?
> If losing it would hurt you — stick to lower risk.
> If you can afford to take a chance — a little higher risk is fine."

**The Golden Rule: DIVERSIFICATION**

Never put all your coins in ONE investment.

Spread your coins across DIFFERENT types of investments.
That way, if ONE fails — you don't lose everything.

🦉 Owl Olivia says:
> "I once put ALL my savings acorns in a single oak tree. One autumn,
> a storm knocked the tree down. I lost everything. 🌳💨
> After that, I spread my acorns across TWELVE trees.
> One tree fell — but eleven were fine. I was fine.
> That's diversification. Don't make my mistake."

**Rocky's Broken Promise 🦝**

Rocky once whispered to every resident: "Put ALL your coins in my new stall —
GUARANTEED 50% return in one week! No risk at all!"

🦉 Owl Olivia's response:
> "STOP. There are two lies in that sentence.
> Lie 1: 'Guaranteed' — nothing in investing is guaranteed.
> Lie 2: 'No risk' — if there's reward, there is ALWAYS some risk.
> Anyone promising guaranteed high returns with no risk is either
> mistaken or running a scam. Walk away. Every time."

## LESSON L9: "Compound Interest is Magic"
**ID:** l9-compound-interest
**Age Band:** 13-18
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Mentor:** 🦉 Owl Olivia
**Prerequisites:** l7-what-is-a-stock, l6-what-is-a-budget
**Chapter Tie-In:** Chapter 5, Beat 5-D
**Concept Tags:** compound-interest, time-value-of-money, investing, starting-young, exponential-growth
**Badge:** 🌱 "Compound Champion"

---

### OPENING DIALOGUE

🦉 Owl Olivia says:
> "Settle in. 🦉 What I'm about to show you is the single most powerful
> mathematical concept in all of personal finance. 
> It is not complicated. It is not secret. But most people don't fully 
> understand it until their 40s — when it's too late to use it at full power.
> You are learning it NOW. That is a tremendous gift.
> The concept is called COMPOUND INTEREST. And it is extraordinary."

---

### LESSON CONTENT

**First: What is Simple Interest?**

Simple interest = you earn a fixed percentage of your ORIGINAL deposit each year.

Example: You deposit 1,000 coins. The interest rate is 10% per year.

Year 1: 1,000 × 10% = 100 coins earned → total: 1,100 coins
Year 2: 1,000 × 10% = 100 coins earned → total: 1,200 coins
Year 3: 1,000 × 10% = 100 coins earned → total: 1,300 coins

Simple interest = same 100 coins every year. Steady, but slow.

**Now: What is COMPOUND Interest?**

Compound interest = you earn interest on your ORIGINAL deposit PLUS all the 
interest you've already earned.

Your interest earns interest. Your gains GAIN gains.

Same example: 1,000 coins at 10% per year, COMPOUNDED.

Year 1: 1,000 × 10% = 100 earned → total: 1,100 coins
Year 2: 1,100 × 10% = 110 earned → total: 1,210 coins ← earned MORE than year 1
Year 3: 1,210 × 10% = 121 earned → total: 1,331 coins ← even more!
Year 5: → total: 1,611 coins
Year 10: → total: 2,594 coins
Year 20: → total: 6,727 coins
Year 30: → total: 17,449 coins

You started with 1,000. After 30 years of compound growth at 10%: 17,449 coins.
That is 17× your original deposit — without adding a single extra coin.

🦉 Owl Olivia says:
> "The quote 'compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world — those who 
> understand it earn it, those who don't pay it' has been attributed to Albert Einstein,
> though this cannot be conclusively verified historically. [UNVERIFIED ATTRIBUTION]
> But whether Einstein said it or not — the MATHEMATICS is undeniable.
> Let the numbers speak for themselves."

**The Secret Ingredient: TIME**

Compound interest needs three things:
1. 💰 Principal (the coins you start with)
2. 📈 Interest rate (the percentage earned per period)
3. ⏰ TIME (the most powerful ingredient)

Watch what time does to 1,000 coins at 7% annual return:

| Start Age | End Age | Years | Value |
|-----------|---------|-------|-------|
| 13 | 65 | 52 years | ~33,000 coins |
| 18 | 65 | 47 years | ~23,000 coins |
| 25 | 65 | 40 years | ~15,000 coins |
| 35 | 65 | 30 years | ~7,600 coins |

SAME principal. SAME rate. Just different starting ages.
Starting at 13 vs. 35: the 13-year-old ends up with FOUR TIMES more coins.

🦉 Owl Olivia says:
> "Every year you wait to start = enormous long-term cost. 
> This is the financial case for starting YOUNG.
> You do not need to be rich to start. You need to START."

**The Rule of 72**

A quick shortcut to estimate compound growth:

72 ÷ your interest rate = years to DOUBLE your money

At 6% annual return: 72 ÷ 6 = 12 years to double
At 9% annual return: 72 ÷ 9 = 8 years to double
At 12% annual return: 72 ÷ 12 = 6 years to double

Source for Rule of 72: This is a well-established financial approximation used in 
standard finance education. Cited in NGPF curriculum and CEE educational materials.
URL: https://www.ngpf.org/

**Compound Interest Works AGAINST You Too**

This same force works on DEBT.

Credit card debt at 20% interest, compounded monthly:
You owe 1,000 coins. You make no payments.

Year 1: you owe 1,200 coins
Year 2: you owe 1,440 coins
Year 3: you owe 1,728 coins
Year 5: you owe 2,488 coins

🦉 Owl Olivia says:
> "Compound interest is extraordinary when it works FOR you.
> It is destructive when it works AGAINST you.
> Understand it. Use it wisely. Never let high-interest debt compound unchecked."

## LESSON L10: "Spotting a Scam"
**ID:** l10-spotting-a-scam
**Age Band:** 13-18
**Duration:** 12 minutes

**Prerequisites:** l5-where-does-money-come-from

---

### OPENING DIALOGUE

🦝 Rocky says (reformed mode):
> "Hey. Yeah, it's me. Rocky. 🦝 Former bank robber. Current Scam-Buster Trainer.
> Look — nobody knows scams better than someone who used to RUN them.

> Source: FTC Consumer Sentinel Network Data Book 2023. 
> URL: https://www.ftc.gov/reports/consumer-sentinel-network

### LESSON CONTENT

**What is a Scam?**

A scam is a deceptive trick designed to take your money, information, or identity.

Scammers are not always obvious criminals. They are often friendly, professional, 
and very convincing. Their goal: make you act FAST before you think.

The one universal rule of all scams: **they need YOU to act before you pause.**

🦝 Rocky says:
> "I used to tell people: 'LIMITED TIME OFFER! Only 5 left! ACT NOW!'
> Why? Because if you took even 30 seconds to think — you'd realise it was fake.
> The URGENCY was the trick. It still is."

**THE 6 SCAM PATTERNS — Rocky's Classified Files**

**Pattern 1: The "Too Good to Be True" Offer** 💰
- "Guaranteed 500% return in 24 hours!"
- "Win a free phone — just click here!"
- "Work from home — earn 10,000 coins per week, no skills needed!"

RED FLAGS: Guaranteed returns. Unrealistic amounts. No explanation of HOW.
RULE: If the reward sounds extraordinary for zero effort — it isn't real.

🦝 Rocky says:
> "This was my first trick. I'm ashamed to admit how well it worked.
> People WANT to believe in easy money. That's the weakness I exploited.
> Don't let hope bypass logic."

---

**Pattern 2: The Phishing Message** 🎣
- An email or message PRETENDING to be from a trusted source.
- "URGENT: Your account is at risk. Click here to verify NOW."
- The message looks official — logo, colours, professional tone.
- But the sender address has a small typo: "suppport@bank.com" (extra p).
- The link goes to a FAKE website that steals your login details.

RED FLAGS: Urgency + unexpected message + requests login credentials or personal info.
CHECK: Hover over (or long-press) any link before clicking. Check the full sender address.
RULE: Legitimate organisations NEVER ask for your password or full account info via email.

🦝 Rocky says:
> "My most sophisticated scheme. I spent THREE DAYS making a fake 
> Mayor Mochi email. Perfect logo. Perfect colours. One tiny typo in the address.
> The kid noticed. I got caught. ALWAYS check the sender address. Always."

---

**Pattern 3: Impersonation** 🎭
- Someone pretending to be an authority: a bank, a government office, tech support.
- "This is the Coinwood Tax Office. You owe back-taxes. Pay immediately or be arrested."
- "This is Apple Support. Your device has been hacked. Give us your password."

RED FLAGS: Unexpected contact. Threat of arrest, fines, or account closure. Requests payment in gift cards, cryptocurrency, or wire transfer. 
RULE: Hang up. Call back the official number you find independently. Never on a number they give you.

---

**Pattern 4: The Pump and Dump (Investment Scam)** 📈📉
- A "hot tip": "Buy CORNWOOD stock NOW — it's about to explode!"
- The tipster already owns lots of shares. As others buy, the price rises.
- The tipster then DUMPS all their shares at the peak.
- Price crashes. Everyone else loses their investment.

Source: The Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) has documented this as 
"pump and dump" manipulation, illegal under US securities law.
URL: https://www.sec.gov/investor/pubs/pump.htm

RED FLAGS: Unsolicited investment tips. Pressure to act immediately. Promise of quick huge gains.
RULE: Research every investment independently. Never invest based on a tip from someone with a financial stake in your decision.

---

**Pattern 5: The Romance / Friend Scam** 💌
- A new "friend" or romantic contact online, often building trust over weeks or months.
- Eventually asks for money: "I'm in trouble. I just need a loan. I'll pay you back."
- Or: "I have an investment opportunity only available to close friends..."

RED FLAGS: Never met in person. Builds trust very quickly. Eventually asks for money.
RULE: Anyone asking for money online whom you've never met in person — stop. Tell a trusted adult.

---

**Pattern 6: The "Advance Fee" Scam** 📨
- "You've won a lottery / inheritance / prize! To claim it, just pay a small processing fee first."
- You pay the fee. The prize never arrives. Scammer disappears.

RED FLAGS: Must pay something to receive something. Unsolicited prize notification.
RULE: Legitimate prizes and lotteries never require upfront payment to collect.

🦝 Rocky says (earnest):
> "I ran versions of every one of these. Pattern 6 was my favourite because 
> people WANTED the prize to be real. They'd pay the fee hoping it was.
> It never was. I regret every coin I took. Don't let anyone do this to you."

**YOUR SCAM-DEFENCE CHECKLIST** 🛡️

Before acting on ANY financial message or offer:

☐ 1. PAUSE. Urgency is a manipulation tactic. 30 seconds of thought is your armour.
☐ 2. VERIFY THE SOURCE. Look up the official number/address independently.
☐ 3. CHECK THE SENDER. Full email address? Tiny typos? Wrong domain?
☐ 4. ASK: "What do they GAIN?" Follow the money trail.
☐ 5. TELL A TRUSTED ADULT. Fresh eyes catch what emotions miss.
☐ 6. NEVER SEND MONEY under pressure. Legitimate orgs wait.

🐶 Mayor Mochi says:
> "And if you're ever unsure? Come to me. 🐶 I will NEVER send you an urgent
> message asking you to verify anything by clicking a link.
> If you get a message that says it's from me — come find me in person.
> That's the Coinwood rule. And it should be your real-world rule too."
